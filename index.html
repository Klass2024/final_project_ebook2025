About Dr. Kaboré

Dr. Lassané Kaboré is a senior public health professional with over 15 years of experience at the intersection of public health research, health policy, and program delivery, with a focus on infectious diseases and immunization. He is passionate about generating and applying real-world evidence to inform data-driven, evidence-based programmatic and policy decisions.

Dr. Kaboré has extensive expertise in research methodologies, including systematic reviews, clinical trials, epidemiology/pharmacoepidemiology, and biostatistics. His work evaluates the use, effectiveness, impact, safety, and cost of drugs and vaccines.

He began his career in 2007 as a Regional Pharmacy Officer with Burkina Faso's Ministry of Health before transitioning to the international development sector. He has held positions at the Agence de Médecine Préventive (AMP), PATH, and currently serves as a Senior Program Officer, Evaluation and Learning at Gavi, based in Geneva, Switzerland.

At Gavi, he leads learning agendas for multiple vaccines—including malaria, Mpox, Ebola, hexavalent, typhoid conjugate (TCV), and oral cholera (OCV)—through implementation and impact studies conducted by leading research institutions.

Dr. Kaboré holds a PharmD from the University of Ouagadougou (Burkina Faso), a joint MSc in International Health from Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin and Bordeaux School of Public Health (ISPED), an MSPH in Epidemiology from the University of Alabama at Birmingham (USA), and a PhD in Global Health from the University of Geneva (Switzerland).

Bilingual in English and French, he has published 20 peer-reviewed scientific articles and continues to contribute to the advancement of public health through research and policy implementation.
